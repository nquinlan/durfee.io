About Me

I am a student studying a somewhat mangled version of the International Relations degree (I study politics and languages instead of politics and economics) at The Univeristy of British Columbia in Vancouver, BC since 2012. I formerly studied aviation at Metropolitan State Univeristy in Denver, CO for two years prior.

I am absolutely facinated with design of all kinds, big data, and technology and cannot wait to see what sorts of ridiculously cool stuff the future holds. I easily get lost in my work, love coffee, and am still a kid at heart and would like nothing more to fly a spaceship and ride a dinosaur (at the same time, of course). Green is my favourite colour, I like works like "Rad" and "Bonkers", I wish I could have a polar bear as a pet, and space boggles my mind to no end.

I like to do all manner of assorted things. I'm passionate about cooking, flying, travel, languages (natural and computer), graphic design, web design, and typography.

My Work

Or lack there of

Without really knowing it, design has been a rather large part of my life for a very long time and through a somewhat fluke introduction to programming and graphical design, I learned that it is one of the more fun and rewarding things I've done. Since then, I've tried to pick up as many odd projects as school will allow and set about introducing myself to the world of design.

Given the *ahem* extensiveness of my portfolio (and my slightly obsessive organizational habits), I decided to tack on a little case study to each of my projects so far with a little summary of what I did, learned, and how many times I threatened to throw my computer out the window in frustration.
